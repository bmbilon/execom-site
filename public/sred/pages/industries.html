Industries

Every vertical presents different structural challenges.

The decisions that affect SR&ED eligibility vary by industry. Contractor models, IP arrangements, entity structures, and available grant programs differ across verticals—and so do the optimization opportunities.

Where Structural Advisory Is Most Relevant

Companies with complex R&D operations and meaningful capital at stake.

Software and SaaS

Contractor and offshore development teams frequently create eligibility gaps. Restructuring engagement models can ensure the full R&D workforce contributes to eligible expenditure.

AI and machine learning

Complex IP ownership questions, cloud compute classification, and dataset licensing create structural challenges that affect both eligibility and audit defensibility.

Hardware and IoT

Prototyping, materials R&D, and cross-border supply chains create IP placement issues. Intercompany agreements with manufacturing partners often limit credit eligibility.

Cleantech and energy

A dense grant landscape that compounds when stacked with SR&ED. Most cleantech companies claim only one layer of available programs.

Life sciences and biotech

Rigorous documentation that translates into strong SR&ED evidence—when the entity structure is correct. Holding company and operating company splits frequently cause refundability problems.

Advanced manufacturing

Process R&D, prototyping, and materials innovation with cross-border supply chains that create IP placement challenges and intercompany agreement complexity.

Engagement Profile

When structural advisory is most impactful.

$500K+ annual R&D spend

Enough R&D activity to generate meaningful credits where structural optimization produces material results—not marginal fee savings.

Complex corporate structure

Holding companies, US parents, cross-border teams, multiple entities. The more complex the structure, the more incentive value is typically being left on the table.

Venture-backed or pre-IPO

Non-dilutive capital matters most when equity is expensive. Every dollar of recovered incentive value is a dollar that does not come from dilution.
