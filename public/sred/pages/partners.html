Your clients' R&D structures determine their SR&ED value.

Execom works with venture firms, CFOs, and accounting practices whose clients have meaningful R&D operations. The referral model is structured around shared economics.

For Venture Firms

Non-dilutive capital your portfolio companies are structurally missing.

Your portfolio companies have SR&ED consultants who file claims every year. What no one has examined is the structure underneath: where the IP sits, whether contractor arrangements are eliminating eligibility, whether IRAP and provincial credits are stackable, or whether the corporate design itself is limiting refundability.

Execom works upstream of the claim process. The firm audits the structural decisions that determine eligibility and value—entity design, IP ownership, contractor treatment, intercompany agreements, and grant stacking—then engineers the framework that maximizes the non-dilutive capital layer.

The result is a larger non-dilutive layer in the portfolio company's capital stack. That means less equity dilution per dollar of R&D.

For CFOs and Accounting Firms

SR&ED value is determined before the claim is written.

Your technology clients need SR&ED filed. Their existing consultant handles the claim. What no one has examined is the structure underneath—the entity design, contractor treatment, IP placement, and grant stacking that determine what the claim is ultimately worth.

Execom works upstream of the claim process to engineer the structural framework. The firm does not replace their SR&ED consultant—it makes the consultant's work more valuable by ensuring the structure produces maximum eligible expenditure.

The referral is straightforward: you introduce your client. Execom handles the structural advisory engagement. You earn a revenue share on every successful engagement, every year the client stays.
