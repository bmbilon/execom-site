About EXECOM

The SR&ED industry optimizes claims.
We optimize eligibility.

EXECOM was built by founders and deal architects who saw that the entire SR&ED consulting industry was solving the wrong problem.

The industry focuses on the output.
We focus on the input.

Every SR&ED firm in Canada competes on the same things: eligibility identification, technical narrative preparation, and maximizing refund percentage. That's the entire pitch. And it's the wrong frame.

The claim is the output. The structure is the input. And most companies — including their SR&ED consultants — never look at the structure. They don't ask where the IP lives, whether contractor arrangements are eliminating eligibility, whether IRAP and provincial credits are stackable, or whether the corporate design itself is limiting refundability.

By the time a consultant prepares the SR&ED documentation, the decisions that control eligibility, refund size, and audit risk have usually already been made. Entity structure, IP ownership, contractor treatment, intercompany agreements, and grant stacking all determine what the CRA will ultimately recognize as eligible R&D expenditure.

We built EXECOM to work upstream of the claim process — engineering the structure that determines what the claim is ultimately worth.
