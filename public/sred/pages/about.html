About EXECOM

SR&ED shouldn't be a
20% tax on innovation

We started EXECOM because the SR&ED industry is broken. Consultants charge 15–25% for templated work, bill extra for audit defense, and lock you into multi-year contracts. We built something better.

The problem is obvious

The Canadian SR&ED program is designed to fund innovation. It puts money back into the hands of companies doing genuine R&D — software, hardware, cleantech, biotech, you name it.

But somewhere along the way, an entire industry grew up around extracting 15–25% of that refund before it ever reaches the companies that earned it. On a $500K refund, that's $75K–$125K going to a consultant for work that, in most cases, follows a predictable, repeatable process.

We looked at that and asked a simple question: what if you standardized the process, focused on clean tech verticals, and passed the savings to the client?

The answer is EXECOM. 5% success fee. $10K minimum. No refund, no fee. That's it.

Aligned Incentives
