About Execom

The SR&ED industry optimizes claims.
Execom optimizes eligibility.

The firm was built by founders and capital advisors who observed that the entire SR&ED consulting industry was solving the wrong problem.

Position

The industry focuses on the output. We focus on the input.

Every SR&ED firm in Canada competes on the same dimensions: eligibility identification, technical narrative preparation, and maximizing refund percentage. That is the entire pitch.

It is the wrong frame.

The claim is the output. The structure is the input. Most companies—including their SR&ED consultants—never examine the structure. They do not ask where the IP sits, whether contractor arrangements are eliminating eligibility, whether IRAP and provincial credits are stackable, or whether the corporate design itself is limiting refundability.

By the time a consultant prepares the SR&ED documentation, the decisions that control eligibility, refund size, and audit risk have usually already been made.

Execom was built to work upstream of the claim process—to engineer the structure that determines what the claim is ultimately worth.

Advisory Principles

Three ideas that govern the practice.

Structure over narrative

Most SR&ED firms write better stories. Execom engineers better structures. The documentation follows—and it is defensible because the underlying R&D design supports it.

Capital architecture thinking

SR&ED is not a tax refund. It is a non-dilutive financing layer. The firm treats it as part of the capital stack alongside equity and debt—and optimizes it with the same rigor.

Aligned economics

5% of recovered credits. $10K minimum. $0 if CRA denies the claim. No retainers, no hourly billing. The firm only earns when the structure it engineered produces value.

Role

Execom works upstream of the claim process.

The firm designs the structural framework that determines eligibility and value. Claim preparation and submission can proceed through a chosen SR&ED specialist, internal finance team, or accounting partner.

Execom is not a replacement for an SR&ED consultant. It is not an accounting firm. It is not a technical writing service.

It is a strategic advisory practice focused on one problem: how should a company be structured so SR&ED eligibility persists and compounds as the business grows?
