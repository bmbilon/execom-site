The SR&ED Leakage Problem

Many companies unintentionally lose a significant
portion of potential SR&ED value

Early structural decisions embed limitations that reduce credit eligibility, sometimes permanently. Common issues include:

🌎

IP in the Wrong Entity

Intellectual property assigned to a parent entity outside Canada eliminates the basis for Canadian SR&ED eligibility.

🏢

Subsidiary Structure Issues

R&D performed in subsidiaries that cannot claim credits results in eligible activity generating zero recoverable value.

🚫

Contractor Arrangements

Contractor arrangements that eliminate eligible expenditures under CRA guidelines — often without the company realizing it.

📈

Grant Interaction Errors

Improper interaction between grants and SR&ED claims can reduce or eliminate refundable credits that should stack.

💰

CCPC Eligibility Limits

Corporate structures that limit refundable credit eligibility through associated company rules or CCPC status problems.

🔍

Embedded Limitations

Once these decisions are embedded in the company's operating model, the opportunity to optimize may be permanently reduced.

Execom's role is to identify and correct these issues early.

SR&ED as a Capital Stack Layer

Founders think about financing
in terms of equity and debt.

For R&D-intensive companies, SR&ED should be treated as a third financing layer.

Equity Capital

Dilutive. Expensive. The capital of last resort for R&D that qualifies for government incentives.

Debt Financing

Covenants and interest. Useful, but not for early-stage R&D. SR&ED should fill this gap first.

SR&ED + Incentives

Non-dilutive. Recurring. When properly structured, a predictable source of capital that reduces dilution and extends runway.

Execom helps companies design their R&D and corporate structure so that this capital layer performs at its maximum potential.
